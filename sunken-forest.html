Bolivia, Salar de Uyuni

Where? Well Bolivia of course.

Bolivia, the home of parades, celebration and culture, housing the largest percentage of indigenous people in South America. The environmental side of Bolivia is unmatched, with such a variety of wildlife and unique species still being discovered to this day. With amazing creatures like the deadly Coral snake, black, brown and yellow in colour. They rarely bite, but when they do, its Toxic. Due to the masses of culture living in Bolivias landscape, the food also follows a certain diversity, with a new delicacy in every town, youll be sure not to have a boring pallet.Cochabamba and La Paz are known for their flourishing restaurants where you can try the traditional Bolivian cuisine. You won't be dissapointed...

Salar de Uyuni is the worlds largest salt flat, "measuring 12,106sq Km and sits at 11,984ft". The unimaginable mirror view forms when there's a little water on the salt surface, matched with the pure white salt flat underneath creates a surreal reflection of the blue sky. Before the beutiful scenes, the Salar de Uyuni was a huge deep lake known as Lake Minchin (30/40,000 years ago to be exact). The lake dried up and left smaller lakes behind which also dried up,leaving 2 salt lakes, Uyuni being the largest. This area has the biggest lithium reserve on our planet up to 70% of the worlds lithium in form of salt is found and exported from here, roughly 25,000 tonnes of salt yearly. Thes massive lithium deposits fuel Bolivia's economy.
