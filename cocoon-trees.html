The devistating floods. 2012.

During Monsoon season, torrential rains flooded canals, farms and infastructure, forcing tens of thousands of people out of their homes. Over 100 were reported dead in Sindh Province, across Pakistan over 400 were killed. Over 15,000 villages were affected and nearly 350,000 were evacuated. In the worst areas of Sindh province waters submerged 4.5 million acres of land, damaging 80% of crops.

The creepy crawleys. On the cotton trees.

The floods of 2012 in Pakistan affected 20 million people, but it also took a tole on Pakistans arachnid population. Over a fifth of ground was covered and submerged making it unhabitable for many creatures. Therefore, millions of spiders chose to climb the trees to escape the rising water, as the flood lasted weeks, the spiders created a cocoon of webs around the trees they climbed, making an eerie phenomenon.

The Brown Widow Spider Checkmate.

The brown widow employs a neurotoxic venom which can cause vomiting, muscle pain and rigidity, however, the bites are not deadly for humans. Female widows are generally the web makers, they prefer to create webs in isolated areas like buckets, mailboxes and house corners.

    This briliant jumping spider often waits upside down at the top of plants to detect pray, once located, she quickly jumps down and captures her target.
