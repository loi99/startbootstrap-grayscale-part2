Abraham Lake, Canada

Abraham Lake is a man made creation, created by the former Calgary Power Company in 1972. The main reason this Lake is so renowned is due to the unbelievable phenomenon which occurs when the Lake freezes over, the trapped methane released from plant decay freezes while rising, creating layers of amazing frozen bubbles under the Lake's surface.


But how are they made?

  • Aquatic plants begin to Decay

    The bottom of the lake houses a variety of plants and tree limbs, these are called aquatic plants. Aquatic plants require special adaptations in order to thrive, as they lack sunlight. Upon time they begin to decay and release methane gas.

  • The plants then release Methane

    The most common place to find Methane would be your stove, or car exhaust. However, aquatic plants release this gas through decay. In this process the amino acid methionine, is released as gas by the decaying plant, this gas then rises.

  • Methane bubbles are formed

    After the aquatic plants decay and release this Methane, it rises in form of bubbles. This process happens as Methane is lighter than air, therefore, these bubbles rise to the surface of the lake, and thus forms our natural phenomenon.

  • The Methane bubbles freeze

    The bubbles filled with Methane rise to the surface of the lake, slowly, the cold temperatures begin to freeze these bubbles, in turn, creating a beautiful natural phenomenon. These bubbles continue to rise and form a sort of stack.
