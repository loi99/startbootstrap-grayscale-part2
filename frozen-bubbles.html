But how are they made?

  • Aquatic plants begin to Decay

    The bottom of the lake houses a variety of plants and tree limbs, these are called aquatic plants. Aquatic plants require special adaptations in order to thrive, as they lack sunlight, these plants combine chemical energy, water and carbon dioxide to make sugar. Upon time they begin to decay and release methane gas.

  • The plants then release Methane

    The most common place to find Methane would be your stove, or car exhaust. However, aquatic plants release this gas through decay. In this process the amino acid methionine, which all living organisms need for the building of proteins, is released as gas by the decaying plant, this gas then rises.

  • Methane bubbles are formed

    After the aquatic plants decay and release this Methane, it rises in form of bubbles. This process happens as Methane is lighter than air, therefore, these bubbles rise to the surface of the lake.

  • The Methane bubbles freeze

    The bubbles filled with Methane rise to the surface of the lake, slowly, the cold temperatures begin to freeze these bubbles, in turn, creating a beautiful natural phenomenon. These bubbles continue to rise and form a sort of stack.
